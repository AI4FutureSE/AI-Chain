Prompt Sapper vs. Prompt Engineering

Prompt Engineering Prompt Sapper
Prompt tricks and patterns, problem decomposition principle AI chain methodology (AI+software engineeering+prompt engineering)
Scattered tools for writing, evaluating and managing prompts Integrated Development Environment for AI chain production

There has been a fast growing literature on prompting engineering, although some people argue that many of them are tricks that can be explained in a few sentences (see the Prompt Engineering blog by Lilian Weng and the Build LLM application for production blog by Chip Huyen). There are many other great online resources that explain prompt engineering tips, such as OpenAI Cookbook, GPT3 Prompts Wikidot, GPT-3 Creative Fiction, Prompt Engineering Guide, as well as systematic literature reviews such as Qiao et al. 2022, Liu et al. 2023, and prompt pattern catalogs such as White et al. 2023, Mishra et al. 2022, The Art of Asking ChatGPT for High-Quality Answers. Besides prompt optimiazation, Decomposed Prompting and Iterated Decomposition have been proposed which leverages the fundamental computational thinking principle - problem decomposition as an effective means of determining ability boundaries and blind spots of the models.

We believe many prompt writing tricks could be volatile as the model capabilities scale up. However, problem decomposition principle, maxims of conversation and prompt tactics (e.g., prompt committee, reflection, persona) will persist, as they are fundamental to any effective problem solving and communication. Therefore, we absorb these principles and tactics into our AI chain methdology and support prompt engineering in the broad context of software engineering in the Sapper IDE.

A wide range of tools have been developed to support building LLM-based applications. These include agent programming frameworks such as LangChain, Dust, Primer and visual programming tools such as PromptChainer. See our comparison Sapper vs LangChain and Sapper vs. No Code AI. In response to the challenges of prompt design, evaluation and management when creating AI functionality based on foundation models, people have developed many scattered tools, such as PromptSource, Promptable, Dyno, GPTTools, Interactive Composition Explorer, Metaprompt, Visual Prompt Builder, AI Test Kitchen, Prompt Engine, OpenPrompt, but these tools are independent and do not support a coherent AI chain methodology. Our Sapper IDE draws inspirations from many of these projects, and makes the world-first IDE for AI chain production.
